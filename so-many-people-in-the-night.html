So Many People In The Night

2026, solo show / installation/ drawings at Cubicle Everard Read alongside Kutti Collective, Mbulelo Lokoto, Kamva Matuis, Turning into Flowers and Aaron Philander. 12-24 January 2025, held at CIRCA , Everard Read, Cape Town.

So many people in the night is a line from the song Slums (1988) by Senyaka. It is an upbeat kwaito-style song about loneliness and the possibility of connection: “many more want to sing with me” . Senyaka sings about how his environment renders him a diffuse subject. He is a man, he is no one, his shoes and luggage are gone. To me, the song is about desire for transcendence and a hope for connection, despite the hardship of inescapable realities.

I am interested in the merchandising of pop culture – religion in particular – in posters, small books, and shiny statues. The installation for this body of work consists of a large wooden structure that takes the viewer on a short, vertical pilgrimage. Alongside the walkable sculpture are drawings and a wall of keepsakes or tokens, all hand-drawn, all containing pieces of text from magazines, and all featuring a mountain as a symbol. “If there is no mountain, there is no God” is a thought I had whilst in Amsterdam – a city erected on wooden piles, flat, with only clouds surrounding it. Besides being physical markers, like in Cape Town, mountains are symbols of deep time. In many cultures, going up a mountain is a spiritual practice. In human time, they are markers of the eternal; in geological time, they are ever-shifting.
